Firehouse Cheeseburgers with Crispy Fried Onions, Garlic Potato Wedges & Creamy Ranch Sauce

Dinner

Total Time: 35 min Source: Hellofresh.com

Ingredients

12 ounce Yukon Gold Potatoes

1 teaspoon Garlic Powder

2 tablespoon Sour Cream

1.5 ounce Buttermilk Ranch Dressing

7.2 g Frank's Seasoning Blend

Frank's Hot Sauce

10 ounce Ground Beef

¼ cup Monterey Jack Cheese

2 unit Potato Buns

Crispy Fried Onions

Salt

Pepper

4 teaspoon Cooking Oil

Directions

Adjust rack to middle position and preheat oven to 450 degrees.
Wash and dry produce.
Cut potatoes into ½-inch-thick wedges.
Toss on a baking sheet with a large drizzle of oil, half the garlic powder(you’ll use the rest in the next step), and a big pinch of salt and pepper.
Roast on middle rack until browned and tender,20-25 minutes.

While potatoes roast, in a small bowl, combine sour cream, ranch dressing, and remaining garlic powder
season with salt and pepper.
TIP: If you like things spicy, add a dash of the hot sauce—just be sure to save some for forming your patties!

In a large bowl, combine beef, Frank’s Seasoning, a dash of hot sauce, and salt.
Form into two patties (four patties for 4 servings), each slightly wider than a burger bun.
Heat a large drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium heat.
Add patties and cook until browned and cooked to desired doneness, 3-5 minutes per side.
In the last 1-2 minutes of cooking, top each patty withMonterey Jack; cover pan to melt cheese.
GRILLING ALTERNATIVE: Heat a well-oiled grill or grill pan over medium-high heat.
Add patties and cook to desired doneness (we suggest 3-5 minutes per side, but grills can vary).
In the last 1-2 minutes of cooking, top each patty with Monterey Jack; cover to melt cheese.

While patties cook, halve and toast buns.
Spread cut sides of buns with creamy ranch sauce, then fill with patties and crispy fried onions.
Divide burgers between plates and serve with potato wedges on the side.
